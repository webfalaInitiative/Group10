Statistics and facts about Sexual and Gender Based Violence

Sexual and Gender-Based Violence (SGBV) is a pervasive and complex issue with far-reaching consequences for individuals, families, communities, and societies. Here are some statistics and facts about SGBV

Underreporting and Stigma

SGBV is vastly underreported globally, with many survivors hesitant to disclose their experiences due to fear of stigma, shame, or retaliation.

Impact on Health

Economic Impact

SGBV can have severe economic consequences for survivors, including loss of income, reduced productivity, and increased healthcare costs.

Prevalence

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), globally, about 1 in 3 women have experienced either physical and/or sexual intimate partner violence or non-partner sexual violence in their lifetime.

Child Marriage and SGBV

Child marriage is a form of SGBV, and globally, around 12 million girls are married before the age of 18 each year, putting them at risk of various forms of violence.

Impact on Education

SGBV can significantly hinder access to education. Girls who experience violence may be forced to drop out of school, perpetuating cycles of poverty and inequality.
